## Part 1 · The strategic frame (the deep dive)

### Where the partnership actually goes

The V2-through-V3.0 spec optimizes for a Year 1 partnership: 14 SKUs, ~$1.1M–$2.4M TOJ take, national coverage via 5 Regional Coordinators. That's the deliverable. **But it's not what makes this partnership defensible.**

Three moats are being built simultaneously — and the Priority 2 build should reinforce all three:

**Moat 1 · Data.** Every Pylon event that runs through Digital Scouts + Optimum grading + Live Event Console generates the only multi-year longitudinal youth-football dataset that stitches evaluation + game footage + engagement + recruiting outcome. Nobody else has it. Nobody else can build it retroactively. **This is the moat Pylon Momentum Score sits on.**

**Moat 2 · Cultural.** Pylon owns credibility with 1,261 teams, 117K athletes, 500+ NFL alumni, 4,000+ D1 commits. That equity compounds. The Signature Series films, HBCU Pipeline, and Alumni Network all lean into it. **Overtime has hype; Pylon has record.**

**Moat 3 · Operational.** The TOJ operator brain — the Coordinators, the cartridge/eval layer, the content taxonomy, the sales sequences, the AI Coach — is portable. When this partnership renews in 2028, or when TOJ takes the same infrastructure to another sport (soccer? track? basketball club?), the moat compounds again. **This is why Regional Coordinators are TOJ contractors, not Pylon employees (V3.0 M8).**

### What TOJ owns at the end of Year 3

- **A dataset** nobody else can replicate (3+ years of Pylon-event grade + stat + film + recruiting-outcome data on ~30K unique athletes)
- **An operating brain** (pylon-os) that has been dogfooded across all 5 US territorial regionals + international
- **A regional operator crew** (5 Coordinators + international contract) with real reps across their territories
- **A licensed IP portfolio** — CRM sequences, content taxonomy, Live Event Console, AI Coach, Recruiting Concierge, Momentum Score algorithm
- **Case-study evidence** — case studies from every SKU that convert the next partnership sale
- **Alumni network access** — 500+ NFL alumni + 4K D1 commits routed through TOJ's operating infrastructure

**Optionality created:**
- Sell the same operator brain to another org (Perfect Game, Little League Softball, USA Volleyball, USA Wrestling)
- Turn Pylon Momentum Score into a licensed data feed for broadcasters + gaming + colleges
- Turn AI Coach into a horizontal SaaS across every youth sport
- Continue rolling into international (Japan → LatAm → EU)

**The Priority 2 build should reinforce this optionality.** Every schema, every route, every skill file, every persona should be legible to a future partner reading pylon-os as a template — same discipline hooks-os already carries. That's not extra work; it's naming discipline.

### The 5-year picture

Year 1 (2026): Pylon partnership stands up. 14 SKUs live. First Coordinators onboarded. Optimum sub-agreement signed.

Year 2 (2027): Momentum Score published. Signature Series airs. HBCU Pipeline delivers first cohort. Recruiting Concierge scales past 3% of addressable. City League Fund makes first franchise acquisition.

Year 3 (2028): TOJ takes the operator brain to a 2nd national sport partnership (candidate: club soccer, given US 2026 World Cup runway). Kyron relocates to NYC/Queens. Northeast Coordinator goes deep.

Year 4 (2029): Data Feed goes B2B (broadcasters, EA Sports). AI Coach sells beyond Pylon coaches into open market. Alumni Fund makes first Pylon-athlete investment.

Year 5 (2030): TOJ is the operating OS for youth sports at national scale. Pylon is one of 3–5 sport verticals. The moat is unassailable.

**Kyron in this frame is the operator + owner of a platform business, not a service provider.** The partnership fee schedule is the fuel; the platform is the asset.

## Part 2 · Kyron's 5 creative additions (LOCKED)

### CE-1 · Pylon AI Coach — SKU #15

**What it is:** an AI-powered coach assistant for HS + youth head coaches. Personalized to their program (roster, scheme, upcoming opponents), grounded in Pylon event data + Optimum grading. Delivers: weekly game planning brief, opponent scouting summary, practice plan variants, in-game decision support, post-game debrief.

**Why it matters:** this IS Kyron's AI school portfolio project earning real money. It's the tuition-recouping asset AND a durable SKU. It's also the most legible demonstration that TOJ's operator brain isn't a service — it's a productizable capability.

**Positioning:** the only coach AI trained on real youth football grade data, not a wrapper on ChatGPT with football-flavored prompts.

**Pricing:** $99/coach/month OR $999/coach/season.

**Splits:** TOJ 60% (this is Kyron's build) · Pylon 40% (brand distribution + coach access + Content Sponsor slot on coach dashboard).

**Year-1 estimate:** 200 paying coaches (out of Pylon's ~5K+ HS coaches touched by events) × $999 season = **$199K gross · TOJ ~$120K**.

**Year-3 estimate:** 800 paying coaches × $999 = $800K gross · TOJ $480K. Plus horizontal SaaS expansion candidate for club sports outside football.

**Skill file:** `agents/skills/pylon-ai-coach/SKILL.md` — persona per-coach (loaded with their team's data), Rules (never fabricate stats, never guarantee outcomes, always cite retrieval), Output-format (weekly brief · scouting summary · practice plan · decision support · debrief), Edge cases (thin data on opponent — flag; roster changes mid-season — re-index).

**Schema addition:** `pylon_ai_coach_subscriptions` (coach_email, team_id, tier, active_from/until, stripe_subscription_id) + `pylon_ai_coach_briefs` (subscription_id, brief_type, week, generated_at, delivered_at, engagement_metrics).

### CE-2 · Pylon Momentum Score — the metric that becomes the moat

**What it is:** a proprietary 0–100 longitudinal athlete performance score. Combines Optimum on-field grade + Pylon event volume + measurables trend + game footage volume + recruiting-momentum signal (offer count, camp invites, social lift) + engagement (parent + coach interactions with athlete's profile).

**Why it matters:** the score is only as defensible as the underlying dataset. Nobody else has multi-year Pylon-event data on the same athlete. **This is the moat that makes SKUs #9 (Athlete Subscription), #1c (Combine Book), and #11 (Recruiting Concierge) irreplaceable.**

**Where it lives:** not a standalone SKU. Embedded across:
- SKU #9 Athlete Subscription (Combine tier): every athlete's public Prospect Edge profile displays their Momentum Score with trend chart
- SKU #1c Combine Book: every team's book includes each rostered athlete's current Momentum Score + season delta
- SKU #11 Recruiting Concierge: every outreach email cites the athlete's current Momentum Score + trajectory
- SKU #15 AI Coach: coach briefs surface Momentum Score movement per roster player

**Positioning:** "the definitive youth football momentum indicator." When it becomes accepted lingo, the moat has landed.

**Media leverage:** the Sunday Salvo (V2.5) publishes a weekly "Momentum Movers" list. The Off-Season Signal newsletter runs a monthly deep-dive. Ranking Show YouTube stream discusses top movers.

**Data feed potential (Year 3+):** license the score + trend data to broadcasters, EA Sports, college programs (see CE-8 proposal below).

**Schema addition:** `pylon_athlete_momentum` (athlete_id, measured_at, grade_component, volume_component, measurable_component, footage_component, recruiting_component, engagement_component, composite_score, trend_direction). Recomputed nightly.

**Skill file:** `agents/skills/momentum-score-computation/SKILL.md` — Purpose (compute the score honestly), Rules (never boost the score to please a client, never suppress the score to defend a ranking), Fallback (thin data → "insufficient data — provisional score").

### CE-3 · Pylon Signature Series — SKU #16

**What it is:** narrative-format short films (5–15 min) featuring individual athletes, families, or programs. Weekly, biweekly, or seasonal cadence. Editorial storytelling — not hype cuts. Distinct from Pylon Path docu-series (which is longer-form seasonal arcs).

**Positioning:** the differentiator vs Overtime. **Overtime has hype; Signature Series has weight.** Every film ends with a directive (subscribe to Athlete Transcript, apply to City League Host program, register your team) — earning its place in the funnel.

**Voice:** documentary. Coach-tone. Same voice guardrails as everything else — no LLM tells, no adjective triples, no "the future of football" opens.

**Pricing:** two revenue paths, both sponsor-driven.

- **Episode presenting sponsor:** $75K–$150K per episode. Sponsor gets pre-roll bumper (5 sec) + tail card + mid-roll integration (organic, editorial). Category exclusivity per episode.
- **Season presenting sponsor:** $750K–$1.5M for full season (8–12 episodes). Sponsor named in every episode title card.

**Splits:** Pylon 60 / TOJ 25 / Subject Media 15.

**Year-1 estimate:** 8 episodes × $100K avg per-episode sponsor = **$800K gross · TOJ $200K**. Ramps 2× in Year 2 with season-presenting sponsor structure.

**Cultural equity:** every episode adds to Pylon's editorial reputation. Big Signature Series moments (an athlete's college signing story, a program's rebuild arc, a family's Friday-night-lights story) become referenceable years later. This is what NFL Films is to the NFL — Pylon at scale.

**Skill file:** `agents/skills/signature-series-production/SKILL.md` — casting rules (represented athletes only for anchor athletes, per V2.7 rule), consent workflow, distribution cadence, sponsor-integration handoff.

### CE-4 · Pylon City League Franchise Ownership Fund — structural, not a SKU

**What it is:** rather than TOJ just selling the City League Host Toolkit (SKU #7 at $2K–$5K/host/season), TOJ raises capital (or partners with an existing fund) to **directly own City League operating rights** in 5–10 markets over 3 years.

**Structure:**
- Fund vehicle: TOJ-managed LLC or partnership with a small-check sports investment fund
- Ownership: TOJ operates the City League in each acquired market · pays Pylon a licensing fee per market · retains operating profit
- Franchise scope: registration fees + Team Media Package attach + Coaches + Officials + venue rev share + local sponsor sales
- Year-1 target: 2 franchises operational (Portland + Dallas, aligned to Kyron's presence)
- Year-3 target: 8 franchises · ~$100–$300K/franchise/year revenue

**Why it matters:** solves the capital-efficiency problem of "who actually operates a City League we've licensed?" Toolkit sale is $5K one-time. Ownership is $100K/year recurring. **10× revenue per market · with equity accretion.**

**Governance:** requires a Pylon licensing agreement covering the use of the Pylon brand at TOJ-owned City Leagues. Pylon gets brand licensing revenue + preferred access to acquisition targets Pylon would otherwise operate itself.

**Capital requirement:** ~$500K–$1M for first 2 franchises (venue deposits, initial staff, marketing). Sourceable from: TOJ operating profit, a small sports investment fund, or Pylon corp itself as a portfolio co-investor.

**Skill file:** `agents/skills/city-league-franchise-ops/SKILL.md` — franchise acquisition criteria, market-fit scoring, operating playbook per franchise, revenue attribution rules.

**Open question:** does Kyron want to raise a fund (a distraction to the operator role) or partner with an existing fund (faster · lower Kyron capital exposure)?

### CE-5 · Pylon × HBCU Pipeline — SKU #17

**What it is:** a dedicated recruiting pipeline connecting Pylon athletes to HBCU football programs. Structural upgrade on Recruiting Concierge (SKU #11) with HBCU-specific coach directory, dedicated content series, and sponsor-anchored HBCU showcase events.

**Components:**
- **HBCU Recruiting Concierge** (premium tier of SKU #11): $149/mo OR $749/season/athlete · powered by an HBCU-specific coach directory sourced via Optimum + direct HBCU AD outreach
- **HBCU Showcase events** (1–2/year): Pylon-branded showcase at HBCU campuses (Grambling, Southern, Prairie View, Jackson State, Howard, NCAT) · athletes travel + get graded + get recruited on-site
- **HBCU Signature Series episodes:** anchor season with 2–3 films following Pylon athletes on their HBCU path
- **Sponsor magnet:** Nike (huge HBCU shoe program), State Farm (HBCU tour, HBCU Classic sponsor), Coca-Cola (HBCU Classic), Walmart (community). Any one of these is a $250K–$1M sponsor conversation.

**Cultural moat:** **serves a market that will remember who showed up first.** HBCU athletics has been under-served by white-owned youth football orgs. Pylon showing up structured, respectful, and with real infrastructure is the kind of thing that gets remembered for decades.

**Split:** HBCU Recruiting Concierge: TOJ 45 / Optimum 25 / Pylon 30 (same as #11). HBCU Showcase: Pylon 60 / TOJ 25 / Subject Media 15.

**Year-1 estimate:**
- HBCU Recruiting Concierge subscribers: 500 × $749 = $375K gross · TOJ $169K
- HBCU Showcase presenting sponsor: 1 event × $500K = $500K gross · TOJ $125K
- Signature Series HBCU episodes: 2 × $100K = $200K gross · TOJ $50K
- **Year-1 total: ~$1.075M gross · TOJ ~$345K**

**Sponsor call priority:** Nike Portland (Kyron will be based there — walk-in) → State Farm (national HQ Bloomington, IL — Kyron travels through Midwest) → Coca-Cola (Atlanta HQ — aligned to Southeast Coordinator).

**Skill file:** `agents/skills/hbcu-pipeline/SKILL.md` — HBCU coach directory sourcing rules, showcase venue selection, sponsor cadence, cultural voice guardrails (never speak for HBCU communities without HBCU voices on the call), consent rules.

## Part 3 · Claude's proposed creative additions (require Kyron greenlight)

Four proposed additions, ranked by strategic priority. Kyron picks which get pulled into the Memo before Priority 2 kicks off.

### CE-6 (PROPOSED) · Pylon AI Highlight Reel Subscription — SKU #18 candidate

**Priority:** HIGH · complements #12/13/14 with a scale-first AI tier at low price point · unlocks the 117K athlete base.

**What it is:** AI-produced per-athlete highlight reels from every Pylon event they play. Automated cut from the full game footage using Optimum grading + play-by-play data to identify their moments. Unlimited highlights per season for one subscription price.

**Difference from Player Feature ($199 human-produced):** AI-generated, unlimited scale, per-play, per-athlete, low price. Different value prop — human-produced is prestige; AI-produced is coverage.

**Pricing:** $29/season/athlete (basic — top 3 plays per event) OR $99/season/athlete (season-long unlimited highlights + game reels).

**Split:** TOJ 55 / Pylon 35 / Subject Media 10 (SM's cut for footage sourcing).

**Year-1 estimate at 10% conversion:** 12K athletes × $29 = $348K gross · TOJ $190K. At 15% conversion on the $99 tier: 5K × $99 = $495K · TOJ $272K.

**Why now:** the technology exists. The footage is already being shot. This is a $500K/year revenue line with near-zero incremental production cost. Pure margin.

**Risk:** cannibalizes Player Feature #14 ($199) at the lower-price end. Position by naming clearly — AI highlights are a base coverage product, Player Feature is a premium editorial product. Different buyer intent.

### CE-7 (PROPOSED) · Pylon Alumni Network + Investment Circle — SKU #19 candidate

**Priority:** HIGH · cultural moat · sponsor magnet · complements HBCU Pipeline · unique to Pylon (Overtime doesn't have it).

**What it is:** 500+ NFL alumni + 4,000+ D1 commits organized into a structured membership network with three surfaces:

1. **Alumni membership** ($299/year) — private community, quarterly newsletter, alumni-only events, mentorship pipeline to current Pylon athletes
2. **Alumni × current-athlete mentorship pairings** — structured mentorship + sponsor amplification (Nike/State Farm love this)
3. **Investment Circle** — accredited alumni pool capital to invest in Pylon athlete NIL deals + brand collabs · TOJ operates the circle, takes management fee

**Revenue:**
- Membership: 500 alumni × $299 = $150K
- Sponsor brand collabs on alumni content: $50K × 10 = $500K
- Investment Circle management fee: 2% of $500K committed = $10K (Year 1; grows to $50K+ by Year 3)
- **Total Year-1: ~$660K gross · TOJ ~$220K at 33% weighted split**

**Why it matters:** cultural moat. **Overtime doesn't have this.** Rivals doesn't have this. Nobody has structured access to 500+ NFL alumni + 4K D1 commits with a mentorship + brand + investment layer on top.

**Sponsor angle:** Nike wants NFL alumni content. State Farm wants Alumni Bowl-style events. Any Fortune 500 brand wants a mentorship-story angle. This is a Pylon-only asset.

**Governance:** Alumni Circle needs a formal governance structure — advisory board, meeting cadence, code of conduct. Legal + compliance overhead in Year 1.

### CE-8 (PROPOSED) · Pylon Data Feed (B2B licensed data) — SKU #20 candidate

**Priority:** MEDIUM-HIGH · highest-margin proposed addition · defensible · uses data TOJ is already generating.

**What it is:** licensed data feed of Pylon grade + stat + ranking + Momentum Score data, sold B2B to:

1. Broadcasters (Fox Sports HS, ESPN+, TruTV, Bally Sports) — for HS football broadcast overlays
2. Gaming (EA Sports College Football 26+) — for youth football pipeline data on future college recruits
3. College programs (recruiting departments) — as a scouting data source
4. Fantasy operators (DraftKings HS, PrizePicks HS) — for youth football fantasy games

**Positioning:** like Elias Sports Bureau for youth football. Structured, licensed, updated in near-real-time. Nobody else has this dataset.

**Pricing:** $50K–$500K/year per licensee depending on scope, refresh cadence, and territorial coverage.

**Split:** TOJ 50 / Pylon 30 / Optimum 20 (Optimum's grade data is a component).

**Year-1 estimate at 3 licensees × $150K avg:** $450K gross · TOJ $225K.

**Why it matters:** highest-margin proposed addition. Requires zero additional Pylon labor — TOJ packages the data feed once, sells N times. Recurring high-margin annuity.

**Risk:** requires legal + IP work to structure the licensing terms. Optimum has to bless the feed (their grade data is a component). Sports Thread has to bless the ranking feed. Both sub-agreements need extending.

### CE-9 (PROPOSED) · The Pylon Book — annual yearbook — SKU #21 candidate

**Priority:** MEDIUM · nostalgic value · parents buy · low operational overhead.

**What it is:** an annual physical + digital yearbook of the Pylon season. Every athlete featured. Every game covered. Every ranking movement documented. Every Signature Series episode indexed.

**Pricing:** $99 physical + digital OR $49 digital-only.

**Positioning:** parents will buy it. It's a keepsake + a credential. **A parent who bought the 2027 Pylon Book will show it to college coaches five years later.**

**Fulfillment:** print-on-demand (Blurb, Mixam, or Amazon KDP), zero inventory.

**Year-1 estimate at 3K sales at $99 avg:** $297K gross · TOJ $118K at 40%.

**Skill file:** `agents/skills/pylon-yearbook-production/SKILL.md` — annual production cycle, athlete-inclusion rules (opted-in only), family-approval workflow for photos, publisher handoff.

**Why medium-priority:** additive but not moat-building. Nice revenue at low operational cost. Skip in Year 1 if bandwidth is tight; add in Year 2.
