## 01 · Executive summary — One page. One decision.

Pylon owns the deepest pipeline receipt in 7v7 football. **500+ NFL alumni. 4,000+ Division I commits. Nineteen years on the field.** No competitor can replicate that graph. Now we're tearing it up and rewriting it again."* That is an organization narrating a pivot without a visible plan.

**The move — Q3–Q4 2026: Publish the alumni graph.** The Pylon Wall (searchable database of every athlete who came through 19 years of Pylon), the annual State of the Pipeline report, and the Alumni Membership tier. One asset. Two pillars fixed at once — Revenue Architecture and Content & Credibility. A story no other 7v7 organization in the country can tell.

**The one thing to fix first:** Revenue Architecture (48/100 — the weakest pillar). The alumni graph is how you fix it without building anything a competitor can copy.

## 03 · Weakest pillar — Revenue Architecture: 48/100.

The pillar that most limits Pylon's separation from competitors is not brand, not content, not operations. It is the money model. Pylon is running a 19-year-old event business without a subscription layer beneath it, and without monetized use of the deepest asset in the organization.

**What's there.** Tournament fees and regional league fees drive the P&L. Multiple products (7v7, camps, flag, showcases, NIL) diversify the event mix. Sponsor mentions appear across regional pages. The transactional infrastructure works.

**What's missing.** Three things. First, no subscription flywheel — every dollar Pylon collects comes from a one-time event registration or a one-time sponsor deal; no recurring revenue rides underneath the event calendar. Second, no published sponsor package structure — deals appear to be negotiated one at a time, which caps deal velocity and inflates negotiation cost per dollar. Third, and biggest: 4,500+ alumni contacts are sitting unmonetized. An NFL alum whose career Pylon helped launch has no formal way to buy back in, refer athletes, or activate against Pylon's youth pipeline in a revenue-share structure.

**Why this pillar loses to competitors.** Red Zone Elite runs on tournament fees too, but its national media coverage and ranking layer sell sponsor packages at scale. PremierEventsUSA layered a player-card partnership on top of fees, creating a per-athlete recurring line. Midwest BOOM landed an apparel sponsor with an annual value that dwarfs individual event revenue. Pylon has assets bigger than any of these organizations own — and is still monetizing at the event-fee layer.

**What fixes it.** The alumni graph. Detailed in Section 04.

## 04 · Cross-pillar move — The Alumni Graph.

One asset fixes two pillars simultaneously: Revenue Architecture (Pillar 03) and Content & Credibility (Pillar 04). Building it also strengthens Brand Identity (Pillar 01) and Growth Readiness (Pillar 05) as a second-order effect. Cross-pillar moves are rare in Foundation Score practice. This is one.

The alumni graph ships as three products under one asset.

### 1 · The Pylon Wall
Public, searchable database of every athlete who came through 19 years of Pylon events. Filter by year, position, region, and current level (HS, D1, NFL). Every profile links to game clips, current-team logo, and social handles.
→ *Fixes Content & Credibility · Brand Identity.*

### 2 · State of the Pipeline
Annual report. Pylon alumni in the College Football Playoff, the NFL Draft, first-round picks, All-American teams, All-Pro selections. A story only Pylon can tell — because only Pylon has the 19-year dataset.
→ *Fixes Content & Credibility · Growth Readiness.*

### 3 · Alumni Membership
Paid tier for D1 alumni and NFL alumni. Revenue share on youth programs their names bring in. Access to a private alumni network. Speaker and clinic opportunities. Formal on-ramp to becoming a Pylon ambassador.
→ *Fixes Revenue Architecture · Growth Readiness.*

**Why it separates 2026.** Red Zone Elite cannot build this — it's four years old. 7v7 Elite cannot build this — it's a rankings layer without the pipeline depth. PremierEventsUSA cannot build this — it doesn't have the alumni. This is the one asset Pylon can build that no competitor can copy without waiting fifteen years. *Different, not better.*
